June 18-19th

Shijie Gu.

I'm feeling 22 in the sun of Boston.

A to-be-scientist actively rowing into the wind.

The wind, now breeze, comes with the memory of the freeze.

The bridges we cross, now in sun, in red green cloudy graffiti,

come with the memory,

Of me and others under the yellow street light,

as well as alone in darkness.

They slip by. They slide away.

Far into the blue sky,

Gone are the traffic sound,

In waves of motion of leg, arm and back,

The giggling of the water,

streaming in the crack of my heart,

With other rowers on the boat.

But with my own oar I row.
